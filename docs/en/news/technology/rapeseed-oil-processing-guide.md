# Rapeseed Oil Processing Guide: Pressing Process, Yield, and Line Setup

*Published: March 9, 2026*
*Category: Technical Knowledge*

Rapeseed oil can serve both mainstream cooking oil demand and regional branded products. For many projects, the main question is how to balance pretreatment, pressing efficiency, and final oil quality in a practical line.

## Common process choice

Hot pressing is usually the more practical route for rapeseed because it supports better aroma and more stable yield. Cleaning, conditioning, heating, pressing, and filtration need to work as one process instead of separate steps.

## What affects yield and line stability

Moisture control, heating consistency, residue separation, and batch discipline all influence both yield and oil appearance. Weak pretreatment often causes unstable output even when the press itself is large enough.

## Equipment planning

Startups can use 300 or 325 series with proper pretreatment, while longer shifts and larger plans benefit from stronger series models. Filtration should also be sized according to the final oil clarity standard.

## Conclusion

A good rapeseed oil project depends on process matching more than on machine tonnage alone. When pretreatment, pressing, and filtration are aligned, both yield and product stability improve.
