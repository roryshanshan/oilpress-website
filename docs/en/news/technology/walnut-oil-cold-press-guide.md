# Walnut Oil Cold Press Guide: Process, Machine Selection, and Packaging Tips

*Published: March 17, 2026*
*Category: Technical Knowledge*

Walnut oil is a premium product usually sold on health positioning, so cold pressing is preferred to protect flavor and sensitive nutrients. Compared with bulk cooking oils, this project depends more on careful raw material handling and packaging.

## Raw material requirements

Use clean, fresh kernels with stable moisture and minimal shell residue. Old kernels oxidize quickly and directly reduce oil quality.

## Typical cold press workflow

The standard flow is sorting, optional crushing, low-temperature hydraulic pressing, settling or fine filtration, and dark bottle filling. Each step should aim to limit heat, air exposure, and contamination.

## Recommended equipment

For regular walnut oil production, 355 to 500 series hydraulic presses are usually more suitable than entry models. Smaller models can still be used for sampling, pilot batches, and budget-sensitive startups.

## Common mistakes

Overheating kernels, overloading the barrel, and storing finished oil in clear containers are common problems. Walnut oil needs both gentle pressing and careful packaging.

## Conclusion

A successful walnut oil project depends on premium raw material, stable cold pressing, and packaging that protects the oil after production. Quality control matters at every step.
