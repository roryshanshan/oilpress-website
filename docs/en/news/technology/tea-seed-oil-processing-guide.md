# Tea Seed Oil Processing Guide: Hot Press, Cold Press, and Equipment Setup

*Published: March 11, 2026*
*Category: Technical Knowledge*

Tea seed oil can be sold as both a traditional cooking oil and a premium regional product. For most buyers, the key decision is whether to target stronger aroma with hot pressing or cleaner premium positioning with cold pressing.

## Typical process flow

A practical flow is cleaning, seed preparation, optional roasting or steaming, hydraulic pressing, filtration, and filling. Hot pressing is common when stronger aroma and higher yield are required, while cold pressing works better for higher-end retail lines.

## Equipment and model suggestions

300 or 325 series can work for hot press startups, while 355 to 500 series are better when the project focuses on steady cold pressing. Pretreatment and filtration should be planned together with the press from the beginning.

## Key quality control points

Pay close attention to seed cleanliness, pretreatment consistency, roasting control, and post-press filtration. Stable raw material quality usually matters more than chasing a larger machine on paper.

## Conclusion

Tea seed oil is a strong hydraulic pressing project when aroma, product quality, and regional positioning are all important. The best result comes from matching process style and machine level to the target market.
