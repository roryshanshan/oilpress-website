# GFP-18A low vacuum filling machine

![GFP-18A low vacuum filling machine](/images/filling-products/gfp-18a-low-vacuum-filling-machine.webp)

`GFP-18A` is an 18-head model in the low-vacuum constant-level filling series. It is suitable for wine and flavoring liquid projects that require higher efficiency than the 12-head model, but do not temporarily require a production capacity of more than 24 heads.

## Core parameters

| Project | Parameters |
| --- | --- |
| Model | GFP-18A |
| Number of filling heads | 18 |
| Reference production | 4500 bottles/hour |
| Total power | 1.12 kW |
| Overall dimensions | 1500×1600×2200 mm |
| Filling method | Low vacuum/constant level filling |
| Applicable liquids | Wine, rice wine, soy sauce, vinegar and other easily foaming liquids |

## What does this device do?

GFP-18A still focuses on fixed-level filling, with the goal of improving the single-machine cycle time while stabilizing the liquid level appearance. For small and medium-sized wine lines, fruit wine lines or glass bottle flavoring liquid lines, it is often used as the first transition configuration when upgrading from 3000 bottles/hour to 4500 bottles/hour.

## Public Features

- Low vacuum liquid level filling structure, suitable for liquids that do not contain air but are prone to foaming during filling.
- The filling liquid flows down the bottle wall, which improves foam control.
- The filling valve has a compact structure and few seals, making it suitable for continuous production.
- The bottle holding action is softer, which can help reduce the bottle loss rate.
- No filling without bottles, which is helpful to improve the stability of subsequent packaging.
- Maintenance and cleaning are more convenient, suitable for routine line replacement and maintenance.

## Which projects are suitable for

- Wine, fruit wine and rice wine projects with a capacity of about 4,500 bottles/hour.
- Customers who want to find a more balanced capacity segment between 12 heads and 24 heads.
- Projects that require filtration, filling, stoppering, and labeling to be combined into a small and medium-sized glass bottle line.
