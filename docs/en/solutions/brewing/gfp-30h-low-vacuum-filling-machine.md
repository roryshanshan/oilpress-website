# GFP-30H low vacuum filling machine

![GFP-30H low vacuum filling machine](/images/filling-products/gfp-30h-low-vacuum-filling-machine.webp)

`GFP-30H` is a 30-head model in the low vacuum constant level filling series. It is suitable for wine, fruit wine, rice wine or glass bottle seasoning liquid projects that have entered the continuous production stage.

## Core parameters

| Project | Parameters |
| --- | --- |
| Model | GFP-30H |
| Number of filling heads | 30 |
| Reference output | 7000 bottles/hour |
| Total power | 2.5 kW |
| Overall dimensions | 2100×2170×2200 mm |
| Filling method | Low vacuum/constant level filling |
| Applicable liquids | Wine, rice wine, soy sauce, vinegar and other easily foaming liquids |

## What does this device do?

GFP-30H is positioned to improve low-vacuum liquid level filling capabilities to a more stable mid- to high-capacity range, while maintaining friendliness for liquids that are prone to foaming. It is often a very practical level for wine customers who need more efficiency and want beautiful and consistent liquid levels.

## Public Features

- Low vacuum liquid level filling, suitable for fixed level filling of gas-free liquids.
- The filling liquid flows down the wall, which can reduce foaming in the bottle.
- The filling valve has a reliable structure and is suitable for continuous operation for a long time.
- The elastic bottle support structure is more friendly to glass bottles.
- No filling without bottles, which helps to stabilize the packaging rhythm in the later stage.
- It is more convenient to disassemble, wash and maintain, which is convenient for daily shift changes and cleaning.

## Which projects are suitable for

- Wine, fruit wine and rice wine packaging line with a capacity of about 7,000 bottles/hour.
- Medium and large glass bottle projects that require linkage with stoppering, cap shrinking, labeling, and packing.
- Customers who want to continue to increase production capacity from the 24-head model, but have not yet reached the configuration of 36 or more heads.
